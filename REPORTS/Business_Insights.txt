# Customer Churn Analysis

## Project Objective

The objective of this project is to analyze customer churn in a telecom company, identify the major factors influencing customer attrition, and provide business recommendations to improve customer retention.

---


## Business Insight 1: Overall Churn Rate

### Finding
The company has an overall customer churn rate of approximately 26.54%.

### Business Impact
More than one out of every four customers leaves the company, indicating a significant customer retention challenge.

### Recommendation
Develop proactive customer retention strategies targeting customers at high risk of churn.

---

## Business Insight 2: Contract Analysis

### Finding
Month-to-month customers have the highest churn rate (42.71%), while customers with two-year contracts have the lowest churn rate (2.83%).

### Business Impact
Customers with short-term contracts are significantly more likely to leave than customers committed to longer contracts.

### Recommendation
Offer discounts, loyalty rewards, or bundled services to encourage customers to upgrade to one-year or two-year contracts.

---

## Business Insight 3: Customer Tenure Analysis
Finding

Customers in their first 12 months have the highest churn rate (47.44%), while customers with more than 49 months of tenure have the lowest churn rate (9.51%). The analysis shows a clear decline in churn as customer tenure increases.

###Business Impact

New customers are at a significantly higher risk of leaving the company. Customer retention improves as relationships become more established over time.

### Recommendation

Implement onboarding programs, personalized engagement, and loyalty initiatives during the first year to improve retention and encourage long-term customer relationships.

---

## Business Insight 4: Internet Service Analysis
Finding

Customers using Fiber optic internet have the highest churn rate (41.89%), compared to DSL customers (18.96%) and customers without internet service (7.40%).

### Business Impact

The Fiber optic customer segment is significantly more likely to leave, indicating potential concerns related to pricing, service quality, or customer expectations.

### Recommendation

Investigate the root causes of churn among Fiber optic customers. Improve service reliability, evaluate pricing strategies, and implement targeted retention campaigns for this high-risk customer segment.

---

## Business Insight 5: Payment Method Analysis

### Finding
Customers using Electronic Check have the highest churn rate (45.29%), while customers using automatic payment methods have the lowest churn rates.

### Business Impact
Manual payment methods appear to be associated with higher customer churn, whereas automatic payment methods improve customer retention.

### Recommendation
Promote AutoPay enrollment by offering incentives and educating customers about its convenience and reliability.

---

## Business Insight 6: Monthly Charges Analysis

### Finding
Customers paying between $60–$89 per month have the highest churn rate (33.74%), closely followed by customers paying more than $90 (32.86%). Customers paying below $30 have the lowest churn rate (9.80%).

### Business Impact
Higher-priced plans experience greater customer churn, suggesting that customers paying more may have higher expectations regarding value and service quality.

### Recommendation
Review pricing strategies and enhance the value proposition of premium plans through additional benefits, loyalty rewards, and improved customer support.
